History

Origins and Development: The Audi R8's story begins with the Audi Le Mans quattro concept car, designed by Frank Lamberty and Julian Hoenig, which was first showcased at the 2003 International Geneva Motor Show. This concept would later serve as the foundation for Audi's first-ever supercar.

Launch and First Generation (2006-2015):
- The production version of the R8 was unveiled at the Paris Auto Show in September 2006, marking Audi's bold entry into the supercar segment.
- The initial model was remarkably faithful to the original Le Mans concept car design.
- The R8 was developed in cooperation with Audi's subsidiary Lamborghini.
- The first model launched was the R8 Coupe 4.2 Quattro.
- The car featured a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission with a V8 engine.

Market Introduction:
- While the car debuted in 2006, it entered the U.S. market in 2008.
- The R8 represented a significant milestone for Audi as it was the brand's first venture into the supercar territory.

Success and Production:
The R8 has proven to be a successful model for Audi, with a total production of 45,949 units.
